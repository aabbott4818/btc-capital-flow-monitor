💡 How Should Investors Interpret the Halving?

Bitcoin's halving cuts the block reward in half approximately every 4 years (210,000 blocks), reducing the rate of new BTC entering circulation. This supply shock has historically preceded significant price appreciation.

Historical Pattern:

  • 2012 Halving (50→25 BTC): Price rose ~9,000% in the 12–18 months following
  • 2016 Halving (25→12.5 BTC): Price rose ~2,800% in the 12–18 months following
  • 2020 Halving (12.5→6.25 BTC): Price rose ~700% in the 12–18 months following
  • 2024 Halving (6.25→3.125 BTC): Current cycle

Key Takeaway for Investors: The diminishing percentage gains each cycle suggest a maturing market, but the pattern of post-halving appreciation has held across all four completed cycles. Many investors use the 12–18 months before a halving as an accumulation window, anticipating reduced supply pressure. The countdown above shows how far we are from the next supply reduction — historically, the period 6–18 months after halving has seen the strongest price action.

⚠ Caution: Past performance does not guarantee future results. Each cycle has unique macro conditions.

📖 The Power Law Model

Bitcoin's price has followed a power law relationship with time since inception: log₁₀(price) = 5.83 × log₁₀(days) + c, where c varies by band. The support floor has never been breached; the resistance ceiling marks cycle tops. Current price relative to fair value indicates the risk/reward zone for accumulation.
